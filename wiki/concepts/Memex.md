# Memex

A hypothetical personal knowledge device proposed by [[Vannevar Bush]] in 1945, in his essay "As We May Think." Conceived as a desk-sized machine for storing books, records, and communications, with the ability to create *associative trails* — links between documents that the user could traverse later.

## Why it matters here

Per [[LLM Wiki Idea]], this wiki is "spiritually descended" from the Memex. The shared DNA:

- **Personal** — owned and curated by one person, not a public commons.
- **Actively maintained** — links between documents are added by the user as understanding develops, not crawled or inferred from scratch.
- **Trails over hierarchies** — the value is in the *connections* between documents, not just the documents themselves.

The web largely went a different direction — public, link-rich but unmaintained per-user, optimized for discovery rather than personal synthesis. The Memex vision is closer to what an Obsidian vault or a personal wiki tries to be.

## The problem Bush couldn't solve

Maintaining the trails — the associative links — is constant work. Bush implicitly assumed the user would do it. In practice, humans abandon personal knowledge bases because the maintenance burden grows faster than the perceived value. This is the gap [[Second Brain]] (as implemented in [[LLM Wiki Idea]]) attempts to close: the LLM absorbs the bookkeeping cost so the trails stay current.
