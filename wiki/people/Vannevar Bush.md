# Vannevar Bush

American engineer and science administrator (1890–1974). Referenced in this wiki as the originator of the [[Memex]] concept (1945), an intellectual ancestor of personal knowledge management systems including the [[Second Brain]] pattern this wiki implements.

## Relevance to this wiki

Per [[LLM Wiki Idea]]:

> The idea is related in spirit to Vannevar Bush's Memex (1945) — a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents. Bush's vision was closer to this than to what the web became: private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves. The part he couldn't solve was who does the maintenance. The LLM handles that.

So Bush's contribution here is the *vision*: personal, curated, link-driven knowledge stores. His contribution's gap was *maintenance*, which LLMs now address.

## Beyond the [[Memex]]

> [!note]
> Only one source so far has discussed Bush, and it focused entirely on the [[Memex]] concept. Bush had a much broader career — directing the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII, shaping postwar U.S. science policy ("Science: The Endless Frontier"), and influencing the founding of the NSF. Worth expanding this page if a future source covers any of that. For now, scope is limited to the Memex thread.
