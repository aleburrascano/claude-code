# Dex Horthy

Founder of **HumanLayer**; author of canonical posts on context engineering for coding agents. The wiki's [[HumanLayer]] page covers the framework; this page covers the voice.

For our wiki, Dex matters because his blog posts are **operational-discipline references** that consistently make a list when practitioners ask "how do I get more out of Claude Code?" The posts are frequently cited across the ecosystem.

## Confirmed canonical posts (verified at humanlayer.dev/blog)

- **"12 Factor Agents"** (April 3, 2025) — foundational principles for production-ready LLM-powered software. Wiki coverage: [[12 Factor Agents (HumanLayer)]].
- **"Advanced Context Engineering for Coding Agents"** (August 29, 2025) — solving complex problems in established codebases. **Strong Tier 3+ ingest candidate.**
- **"Context-Efficient Backpressure for Coding Agents"** (December 9, 2025) — wrapper scripts to reduce token waste. Pattern related to [[rtk (rtk-ai)|RTK's PreToolUse rewriting]].
- **"A Brief History of Ralph"** (January 6, 2026) — documents the [[Ralph Wiggum Technique]]'s emergence; credits [[Geoffrey Huntley]] as originator.
- **"Getting Claude to Actually Read Your CLAUDE.md"** (March 17, 2026) — uses conditional XML blocks to improve instruction adherence. **Direct relevance to [[Memory]].**

## Why his perspective is high-signal

1. **HumanLayer founder.** Building infrastructure for human-in-the-loop agent development — credibility comes from shipping.
2. **The "12 Factor Agents" naming.** Successful pattern-naming move (analogous to 12-Factor App). The framework is referenced widely.
3. **Operational depth.** Posts like "Getting Claude to Actually Read Your CLAUDE.md" surface specific *mechanical* problems and concrete fixes (XML blocks for conditional adherence). Most ecosystem writing stays at the philosophy layer; Dex's writing reaches the wire-level.
4. **Continuity with named patterns.** "A Brief History of Ralph" historiographically anchors the Ralph technique — does the work of naming, attribution, and validation other ecosystem writing tends to skip.
